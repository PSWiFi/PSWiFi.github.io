GBA Proof Guidelines

This page contains information and examples on how to create proof for your Pokémon in Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald & Fire Red/Leaf Green to an acceptable standard for general trading in PS! Wi-Fi. Only information for the above mentioned games is provided on this page.

GBA Overall Requirement:

Pokémon obtained in the above mentioned GBA titles that meet the description of anything listed directly below need proof to trade inside the PS! Wi-Fi room.

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Roaming/Static Encounters & Egg/Gift Redemptions:

  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon

Untradable Pokémon

The following Pokémon cannot be traded in the Wi-Fi room at all:

  • Any Gen 3 Event Pokémon that was not originally distributed via one of the Boat Tickets or Old Sea Map Key Items

Gen 3 Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Gen 3 (GBA) titles. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Obtained in Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald or FireRed/LeafGreen:

If using VBA/BizHawk or a similar app with .lua scripts for RNG abuse you must provide:

  • For Encounters, a screenshot of you hitting your target frame (i.e last button inputs for Wild & Static, or screen of generation for Roamers) and a screenshot of the encounter initiation (i.e the “Pokémon appeared!” screen or similar).

  • For Redemptions, a screenshot of you hitting your target frame (i.e “Player received Pokémon!” screen) and a screenshot of the summary screens cleary showing OT/TID, Nature and IVs.

  • For Eggs, a screenshot of you hitting your target frame (i.e "Would you like to take Pokémon? Y/N" screen or similar) and a screenshot immediately after recieving the egg confirming the stats and internal details.

  • A screenshot of the tool used to RNG abuse the Pokémon. You must show your Seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used.

  • A full uncut video starting from a successful capture or redemption of your Pokémon, leading into the in-box summary screens clearly showing the Stats, Nature, Ability, and OT/TID.
