GBA Proof Guidelines

This page contains information and examples for how to create proof for your Pokémon in Ruby/Sapphire/Emerald & Fire Red/Leaf Green to an acceptable standard for general trading in PS! Wi-Fi. Only information for the above mentioned games is provided on this page.

GBA Overall Requirement:

Pokemon obtained in the above mentioned GBA titles that meet the description of anything listed directly below need proof to trade inside the PS! Wi-Fi room.

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Roaming/Stationary/Gift encounters:

Encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon

Pokémon obtained from eggs:

  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon

Gen 3 Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Gen 3 (GBA) titles. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Stationary, Wild, & Gift Encounters:

If you are using emulator with .lua scripts you must provide:

  • A screenshot or picture of the encounter starting (i.e the “Pokémon appeared!” screen, with the lua output clearly visible on-screen.

  • A screenshot or picture of the Pokemon summary, displaying the stats and OT/TID of the Pokemon that was obtained. You may use the lua script to confirm the IVs of the Pokémon.

  • If you did not show the OT/TID screen, you must show a screenshot of your trainer card instead.

  • A screenshot or picture of the tool used to RNG abuse the Pokémon. You should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used. Sensitive information such as Seed/PID/EC can be partially blurred.

  • For roaming encounters, you must show the frame being hit at the correct screen that the roamer is generated on.

For soft resetting and retail RNG abuse you must provide:

  • A full uncut video starting from a successful capture of your Pokémon leading into the in-box summary screens clearly showing the stats, nature, ability, and OT/TID.

  • A screenshot or picture of the tool used to RNG abuse the Pokémon. You should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used. Sensitive information such as Seed/PID/EC can be partially blurred.

For soft resetting and retail RNG abuse you must provide:
