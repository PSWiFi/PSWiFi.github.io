DS Proofing Guidelines

This page contains information and examples for how to create proof for your Pokémon in Diamond/Pearl/Platinum, HeartGold/SoulSilver and Black(2)/White(2) to an acceptable standard for general trading in PS! Wi-Fi.

In all cases, your PS! Username should be visible somewhere within your proof link. Furthermore, ALL Pokémon must follow the room's requirements for every generation, even if not specifically outlined here. Be sure to review our overall requirements to see if your Pokémon meets the general standard.

As an example, although not directly described on this page, a wild shiny Pokémon with 3 IVs from an encounter with 0 guaranteed IVs will still need proof because this is a requirement for all generations, as described in our overall proofing rules found above.

Minimum Proof Requirements:

The following Pokémon/Encounter types need proof when obtained in ANY Generation 4/5 game:
Wild, Stationary, Roaming, and Eggs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon.
  • 4 IV+ Pokémon.
Events
  • 3 IV+ Pokémon obtained from an event with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • 4 IV+ Pokémon obtained from an event 2+ guaranteed perfect IVs.
  • ALL "sometimes shiny" shiny event Pokémon.
  • ALL "sometimes shiny for traded" event Pokemon from eggs. (e.g Manaphy)

Static, Wild and Roaming Encounters:

RNG using .lua scripts:
You must provide:
  • An in-game picture or screenshot showing a target frame has been hit sucessfully. This image should include .lua output, showing seeds and any other important information.
  • An in-game picture or screenshot that shows the encounter starting (i.e. The “Pokémon appeared!" screen) with the output from the lua script clearly visible on-screen.
  • An in-game picture or screenshot of the Pokemon summary, displaying the stats and OT/TID of the Pokemon that was obtained. You may use the lua script to confirm the IVs of the Pokémon.
  • As this Pokemon was obtained by RNG abuse, provide a screenshot of the tool used to RNG the Pokémon, you should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used (such as Synchronise, Cute Charm, etc.).
  • For Gen 5 RNG using C-Gear, show a picture or screenshot of hitting your C-gear seed.
  • For roaming encounters, you must show the frame being hit at the correct screen that the roamer is generated on.
Retail RNG & Soft Resetting:
You must provide:
  • A full uncut video from hitting your target Pokémon starting from the “Pokémon appeared!” screen in-battle. The video should contain a successful capture showing off all the relevant screens such as the summary and OT/TID.
  • If this Pokémon was obtained by RNG abuse, provide a screenshot of the tool used to RNG the Pokémon, or include the program output inside your proof video. You should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used (such as Synchronise, Cute Charm, method, etc.).

Gift/Mystery Gift redemptions:

RNG using .lua scripts:
You must provide:
  • An in-game picture or screenshot showing a target frame has been hit sucessfully. (i.e The "You redeemed a Pokemon" screen) This image should include .lua output, showing seeds and any other important information.
  • An in-game picture or screenshot of the Pokémon summary, displaying the stats and OT/TID of the Pokémon that was obtained. You may use the lua script to confirm the IVs of the Pokémon.
  • If the Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player (yours), you must also provide a picture of your trainer card. This includes event eggs that are shiny locked to the receiver but can hatch shiny when traded, such as Manaphy.
  • As this Pokémon was obtained by RNG abuse, provide a screenshot of the tool used to RNG the Pokémon, you should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used (such as Synchronise, Cute Charm, etc.).
Retail RNG & Soft Resetting:
You must provide:
  • A full uncut video from hitting your target frame starting from the “You redeemed a Pokémon” screen. The video should contain a successful redemption showing off all the relevant screens, such as the summary and OT/TID.
  • If the Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player (yours), you must also provide a picture of your trainer card. This includes event eggs that are shiny locked to the receiver but can hatch shiny when traded, such as Manaphy.
  • If this Pokémon was obtained by RNG abuse, provide a screenshot of the tool used to RNG the Pokémon, or include the program output inside your proof video. You should show your seed and desired target frame along with any other specific configurations used (such as Synchronise, Cute Charm, etc.).
