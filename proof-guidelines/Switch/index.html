Switch Proof Guidelines

This page contains information and examples for how to create proof for your Pokémon in Scarlet/Violet & Sword/Shield to an acceptable standard for general trading in PS! Wi-Fi. Only information for the above mentioned games is provided on this page.

Click the button directly below this text if you are looking for proofing guides for Let's Go Pikachu/Eevee, Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl or Legends: Arceus.

Click here for a tutorial on proofing in Switch side game titles! (BDSP | LGPE | PLA)

Proofing tips & advice

  1. Pressing the record button on your Switch will record the previous 30 seconds of gameplay footage.

    2. This means you should hold the record button after viewing the summary of your Pokémon to capture the correct 30 seconds of gameplay.

  2. It is advised to unlock the IV judge before attempting to prove a Pokémon, in order to accurately prove the IVs in-game.

    3. If you are unable to provide an in-game IV judge evaluation you must use one of the following methods to prove the IVs of your Pokémon.

    1. 3rd party applications or reader outputs (e.g. The output from an automated encounter obtained by using a bot)
    2. Showdown! TeamBuilder or an online IV calculator, if the IVs are not exact this will invalidate the proofing! Be sure to level up the Pokémon enough to clearly show the exact IVs and then attach the screenshot of the calculator to your proof album.
    3. You may use RaidFinder/RaidCrawler screenshots provided from the host of a Max/Tera raid to prove the IVs of your Pokémon.

    In all situations we advise unlocking the judge where possible to avoid extra complexity.

  3. When a Pokémon is sent to boxes in Scarlet/Violet & Sword/Shield, it will be sent to the first available box slot of the last viewed box.

    4. For this reason it is advised to leave a space in your party, or at minimum a space in the current viewed box for the Pokémon to go to.

  4. Your Pokémon should be easily identifiable within your proof video with no ambiguity whatsoever.

    5. It is advised to send the Pokémon to a party slot, or clear out box to send the Pokémon to before attempting to prove a Pokémon.

Scarlet/Violet Overall Requirement:

Pokémon obtained in Scarlet/Violet that meet the description of anything listed directly below need proof to trade inside the PS! Wi-Fi room.

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Stationary/Outbreak encounters:

Encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon
Encounters with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 5 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV non-shiny Pokémon
  • 6IV or 5IV flawless Legendary Pokémon
Note: Encounters in Generation 9 games with fixed values, such as the box legendaries & titan rebattles do not need proof.

Pokémon obtained from Tera Raid dens:

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV Pokémon
  • This extends to Pokémon in event distribution Tera dens

Pokémon obtained from event distributions:

Distributions with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Distributions with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 6 IV non-shiny/shiny Pokémon

UNTRADABLE:

The following Pokémon cannot be traded in the Wi-Fi room in any capacity.
  • Pokémon hatched from eggs obtained from Link Trades
  • 6IV shiny rare form Tandemaus and Dudunsparce from wild encounters or hatched from eggs
  • Shiny Pokémon from Egg/Raid/Static/Gift encounters that qualify for the Jumbo OR Mini mark
  • Mystery Gift & Static encounters that qualify for the Jumbo OR Mini mark

Sword/Shield Overall Requirement:

Pokémon obtained in Sword/Shield that meet the description of anything listed directly below need proof to trade inside the PS! Wi-Fi room.

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Roaming/Stationary encounters:

Encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon
  • Shiny Pokémon from 1% chance Wild encounters. (e.g Sinistea-Antique)
  • Shiny Pokémon from a Wild encounter with a Rare Mark
Encounters with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 5 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV non-shiny Pokémon

Pokémon obtained from Raids/Dynamax Adventures:

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV Pokémon
  • In dens with boosted shiny chance, only full 6 IV Pokémon require proof

Pokémon obtained from event distributions:

Distributions with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Distributions with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 6 IV non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Note: Event Pokémon from Generation 8 games and HOME with fixed values, such as guaranteed 6IV shiny Eternatus or HOME Melmetal, do not need proof.

UNTRADABLE:

The following Pokémon cannot be traded in the Wi-Fi room in any capacity.
  • Pokémon hatched from eggs obtained from Link Trades
  • 6IV Pokémon from in-life event distributions
  • Shiny Pokémon from Watchtower Max Raid distributions

Scarlet & Violet Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Scarlet/Violet. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Tera Raid Battles:

For Tera Raid Battle proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture leading into in-box IV evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature. The video should start on the raid reward screen. (Starting from the Pokédex pop-up after a raid is also acceptable)
Examples:
  • Tera Raid Battle

Overworld, wild & legendary encounters:

For encounter proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture leading into in-box IV evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature. The video should start before the encounter has ended (i.e the Pokémon was caught! screen or similar).
Examples:
  • Wild Encounter

Sword & Shield Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Sword/Shield. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Max Raid Battles & Dynamax Adventures:

For Max Raid & Dynamax Adventure proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture leading into in-box IV evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature. The video should start on or before the raid reward screen.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved by RNG abuse or botting, you must provide an additional screenshot of the result/output, sensitive information like seeds (no more than half!), PID and EC can be partially blurred.
Examples:
  • Max Raid Battle
  • Dynamax Adventure

Stationary, Wild & Gift Encounters:

For Stationary, Wild & Gift Encounter proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture or redemption leading into in-box IV evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature of the Pokémon you caught.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved by RNG abuse or botting, you must provide an additional screenshot of the result/output, sensitive information like seeds (no more than half!), PID and EC can be partially blurred.
Examples:
  • Stationary
  • Wild
  • Gift
  • Curry (Pokémon Camp)

Unique Cases:

  • Eternatus: Your video portion can start from any portion of the cutscene and must provide all above information. It is impossible to have the IV judge evaluation so you may prove the IVs using a Teambuilder screenshot or similar.

  • Wild Sinistea-Antique: You must provide an additional video showing the blue antique mark on the bottom of Sinistea-Antiques sprite in battle.
Examples:
  • Eternatus
  • Sinistea-Antique

Mystery Gift Redemptions:

For Mystery Gift redemption VIDEO style proofing you must provide:

  • A video of the redemption, clearly showing a valid code has been used, the video must clearly display the IVs and nature of the Pokémon in the box immediately after redemption.

  • If the event Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player, you must show a picture of your trainer card or save screen, as well as the OT/TID summary screens of the Pokémon.

  • A paper slip on display in your videos clearly showing your PS! Username. (Watermark is acceptable for video proofing.)

For Mystery Gift redemption IMAGE style proofing you must provide:

  • A picture of the redemption screen, clearly showing a valid code has been used, along with a picture clearly displaying the IVs and nature of the redeemed Pokémon.

  • If the event Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player, you must show a picture of your trainer card or save screen, as well as the OT/TID summary screens of the Pokémon.

  • A paper slip on display in your images clearly showing your PS! Username. (This means you will need to use an external camera to take the pictures.)
Examples:
  • Mystery Gift (Video)
  • Mystery Gift (Image)
