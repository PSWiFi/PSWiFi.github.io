Switch Side-Game Guidelines

This page contains information and examples for how to create proof for your Pokémon in Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Let's Go Pikachu/Eevee & Legends: Arceus to an acceptable standard for general trading in PS! Wi-Fi. Only information for proofing in the above mentioned games is provided on this page.

Click the button directly below this text if you are looking for proofing guides for Sword/Shield & Scarlet/Violet.

Click here for a tutorial on proofing in Switch main series titles! (SwSh | ScVi)

Proofing tips & advice

  1. Pressing the record button on your Switch will record the previous 30 seconds of gameplay footage.

    2. This means you should hold the record button after viewing the summary of your Pokémon to capture the correct 30 seconds of gameplay.

  2. It is advised to unlock the IV judge before attempting to prove a Pokémon, in order to accurately prove the IVs in-game.

    3. If you are unable to provide an in-game IV judge evaluation you must find an alternative method of proving the IVs of your Pokémon.

  3. When a Pokémon is sent to boxes, it will be sent to the first available box slot starting from Box 1.

    4. For this reason it is advised to leave a space in your party, or at minimum a space in Box 1 for the Pokémon to go to.

Side-game Overall Requirement:

Pokemon obtained in Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Let's Go & Legends: Arceus that meet the description of anything listed directly below need proof to trade inside the PS! Wi-Fi room.

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Roaming/Stationary encounters:

Encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon
  • Shiny Pokémon from 1% chance Wild encounters.
Encounters with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 5 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV non-shiny Pokémon

Pokémon obtained from chaining (LGPE/BDSP):

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV Pokémon
  • Wild shiny Legendary birds from overworld-based encounters in LGPE

Pokémon obtained from event distributions:

Distributions with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Distributions with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 6 IV non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Note: Event Pokémon from Generation 8 games and HOME with fixed values, such as guaranteed 6IV shiny Eternatus or HOME Melmetal, do not need proof.

UNTRADABLE:

The following Pokémon cannot be traded in the Wi-Fi room in any capacity.
  • Pokémon hatched from eggs obtained in Link Trades
  • 6IV Pokémon from in-life event distributions

BDSP Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Stationary, Wild & Gift Encounters:

For Stationary, Wild & Gift Encounter VIDEO proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture or redemption leading into in-box IV evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature of the Pokémon you caught.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved by RNG abuse or botting, you must provide an additional screenshot of the result/output, sensitive information like seeds, PID and EC can be partially blurred.

  • If your Pokémon is from a PokéRadar chain, you must provide an additional screenshot of the chain length as displayed on the Pokétch
Video Examples:
  • Stationary
  • Wild
  • Gift
  • PokéRadar Shiny

For Stationary, Wild & Gift Encounter CFW IMAGE proofing you must provide:

  • For Stationary/Wild encounters, you should include screenshots from the overlay page that clearly displays the IVs, EC and PID of your Pokémon during battle.

  • For in-game gift redemptions, you should include screenshots from the overlay page that clearly displays the IVs, EC and PID of your Pokémon immediately after redemption.

  • In all cases you should include screenshots from the same overlay page on the summary screen of your Pokémon, clearly showing your OT/TID.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved by RNG abuse or botting, you must provide an additional screenshot of the result/output, sensitive information like seeds, PID and EC can be partially blurred.

  • If your Pokémon is from a PokéRadar chain, you must provide an additional screenshot of the chain length as displayed on the Pokétch
Image Examples:
  • Stationary/Gift
  • PokéRadar Shiny

Mystery Gift Redemptions:

For Mystery Gift redemption VIDEO style proofing you must provide:

  • A video of the redemption, clearly showing a valid code has been used, the video must clearly display the IVs and nature of the Pokémon in the box immediately after redemption.

  • If the event Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player, you must show a picture of your trainer card or save screen, as well as the OT/TID summary screens of the Pokémon.

  • A paper slip on display in your videos clearly showing your PS! Username. (Watermark is acceptable for video proofing.)

For Mystery Gift redemption IMAGE style proofing you must provide:

  • A picture of the redemption screen, clearly showing a valid code has been used, along with a picture clearly displaying the IVs and nature of the redeemed Pokémon.

  • If the event Pokémon takes the OT/TID of the player, you must show a picture of your trainer card or save screen, as well as the OT/TID summary screens of the Pokémon.

  • A paper slip on display in your images clearly showing your PS! Username. (This means you will need to use an external camera to take the pictures.)

For Mystery Gift CFW OVERLAY style proofing you must provide:

  • A screenshot of the redemption screen showing a valid code has been used, with the overlay visible clearly displaying the IVs and nature of the redeemed Pokémon.

  • A screenshot of the in-box summary screen showing the OT/TID of the redeemed Pokémon, with the overlay visible again clearly displaying the IVs and nature of the redeemed Pokémon.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved using RNG abuse, you must provide a screenshot of the tool used displaying your seed, frame count, IVs, PID and EC. It is encouraged to partially blur the seed, PID and EC.

  • For the BDSP Manaphy egg specifically, you must provide an uncut video of the egg hatching, leading into IV judge evaluation and showing the summary pages. (It's possible to save before hatching the egg!)
Examples:
  • Mystery Gift (Video)
  • Mystery Gift (Image)
  • Mystery Gift (Overlay)

Legends: Arceus Proofing

This section of the page covers how to create proof for Pokémon obtained in Legends: Arceus. In all cases, your PS! Username must be visible somewhere within your proof link so that we can easily verify exactly where the Pokémon has come from.

Stationary, Overworld & Massive Mass Outbreaks:

For Stationary, Overworld, & Outbreak Encounter proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture, leading into in-box Effort Level evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature of the Pokémon.

  • Your Pokémon must be sent to the party. Ensure you have a slot available for it to go to. This is due to the the lack of a remote-access box system.

  • If your Pokémon was achieved by RNG abuse, you should include screenshots of tools used to further prove IVs. You may also use overlays such as capturesight accompanied with a proof video to prove the IVs.

  • Disable autosave to re-encounter most Pokémon in the overworld. Use this to your advantage and save upon encountering any Pokémon you believe may need proof, and you can then adjust your party accordingly.

For Stationary, Overworld, Gift & Outbreak Encounter proofing you must provide:

  • A consistent video of a successful capture or redemption leading into in-box Effort Level evaluation and summary screens, enough to clearly show OT/TID and nature of the Pokémon.

  • Your Pokémon must be sent to the party. Ensure you have a slot available for it to go to. This is due to the the lack of a remote-access box system.

  • Some scripted/legendary encounters have an involved cut-scene, in the event of this you may start your video recording from any point during the cut-scene.

  • Tips, tricks and advice for proofing scripted/legendary encounters can be found in the Scripted/Legendary Encounters example directly below.
Examples:
  • Wild Encounter
  • Mass Outbreak Shiny Encounter
  • Scripted/Legendary Encounters
