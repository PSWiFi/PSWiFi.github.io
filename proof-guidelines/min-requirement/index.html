Proofing Guidelines

“Proof" refers to pictures or videos that verify that a Pokémon has been legitimately obtained by a player, and helps to reduce the chance that a Pokémon is hacked. Proof of Pokémon you trade should be readily available upon staff request; failure to provide proof will potentially be met with punishment.

The Wi-Fi room has minimum proof requirements for certain "rare" Pokémon. These requirements can vary depending on IVs and shininess, as well as the generation or game the Pokémon was caught in.

It is advised to partially blur sensitive information on your proof screenshots and videos (such as initial seeds) to prevent people from stealing your proof and / or cloning your Pokémon. You may partially blur initial seeds and PIDs to avoid this from happening.

Wi-Fi’s Proofing Guidelines are not up for debate in the Wi-Fi room.

If you have a strong argument that our proof guidelines are lacking or that a section of it should be revisited, kindly PM a member of our staff team (% and up). The final decision remains at the discretion of Wi-Fi staff.


Examples of "Flawless" or "Perfect" IV spreads:

Note: IV spreads are ordered in sequential order of HP / Atk / Def / SpA / SpD / Spe. 'x' means the IV does not need to be perfect, and can be anything below perfect. The Wi-Fi room defines 0, 1, 30 and 31 IVs as “Perfect”, and so a Pokémon will need proof if they meet these requirements; this includes specifically targeting 30 IVs over 31 IVs for Hidden Power types. The one exception to this rule is Legends: Arceus, where 0, 1, 30, and 31 IVs are not noted on the Pokémon's summary as there is no in-game IV judge.

  • Examples of perfect IVs for physically-based Pokémon –
    • 31/31/31/x/31/31 5 perfect IVs + Adamant nature; perfect IVs + nature for a physical attacker.
    • 31/31/31/x/31/0 5 perfect IVs + Adamant nature; built for Trick Room.
      • Note: As 30 and 31 are both defined as perfect IVs, any of these stats with 30 IVs instead of 31 IVs still require proof.
  • Examples of perfect IVs for specially-based Pokémon –
    • 31/0/31/31/31/31 6 perfect IVs + Timid nature; perfect IVs + nature for a special attacker.
    • 31/0/31/31/31/0 6 perfect IVs + Quiet nature; built for Trick Room.
      • Note: As 30 and 31 are both defined as perfect IVs, any of these stats with 30 IVs instead of 31 IVs still require proof.
  • Other examples of perfect IVs –
    • 0/0/0/0/0/0 6 perfect IVs Gen 5 Ditto; even all 0s constitues as 6 IVs!
    • 31/31/15/x/31/0 5 perfect IVs + Lonely nature; Trick Room Stakataka optimized for Beast Boost to boost Attack.
IN LEGENDS: ARCEUS ONLY: Only full 31 IVs are considered towards total flawless IV count. This will be displayed by an initial effort level of 3 in a stat after capture. For example, a Pokémon caught with 6 effort levels at 3 would be considered a 6 IV Pokémon.

Does my Pokémon need proof?

This portion of the document covers the types of Pokémon that require proof in each generation. If your Pokémon meets the standard of anything listed below, you must be able to provide proof to trade them in our room.

All Generations
    • Pokémon obtained from Wild Encounters (including roaming Pokémon).
      • 3 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a wild encounter with 0 guaranteed IVs.
      • 4 IV+ Pokémon from a wild encounter with 0 guaranteed IVs.
    • Pokémon obtained from Stationary Encounters (for example, starters, static Legendary Pokemon, and fossils).
      • 3 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 0 guaranteed IVs.
      • 4 IV+ Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 0 guaranteed IVs.
      • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 3 guaranteed IVs.
      • 6 IV Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 3 guaranteed IVs.
    • Pokémon obtained from Events.
      • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon from an event distribution with 0 guaranteed perfect IVs.
      • 6 IV Pokémon, both shiny-locked and guaranteed shiny, from an event distribution with 3 guaranteed perfect IVs
    • Pokemon obtained through hatching a shiny Pokémon due to matching TSV values traded between two users require proof.
      • TSV hatched Pokémon must provide a screenshot of the conversation between the two users, agreeing upon a TSV hatch.
Generation 8:
This includes: Sword/Shield, Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Let's Go! Pikachu/Let's Go! Eevee, and Legends: Arceus.

All Generation 8 Titles:
  • 3 IV+ Pokémon from global event distribution codes with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • 5 IV+ Pokémon from global event distribution codes with 3 guaranteed IVs.
  • All Pokémon from unique code event distributions.
  • Shiny Pokémon from 1% chance encounters.
A Global event distribution hands out the same code for all players to redeem the Pokémon; this can be shared amongst players and reused. Typically you can use these codes once per save file.

A Unique event distribution hands out unique codes for players to redeem Pokémon; these are one-time-use and cannot be shared around. They also typically require an in-life action to obtain one.

Event Pokémon, such as HOME distributions, with fixed IVs/shininess do not need proof.

Sword/Shield Specifics:
  • Shiny Pokémon from a Wild Encounter with a Rare Mark.
  • 3 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a Wild Encounter.
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from promotional Max Raid Dens with a boosted shiny chance.
Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl Specifics:
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from PokéRadar chains.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from PokéRadar chains.
Let's Go! Pikachu/Let's Go! Eevee Specifics:
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • Wild Shiny Articuno, Zapdos, and Moltres from Overworld-based encounters.
Legends: Arceus Specifics:
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from encounters in the Overworld, Space-Time Distortions, or (Massive) Mass Outbreaks.
  • Flawless Legendary and Mythical Pokémon (flawless is considered perfect 5 IV+ in Legends: Arceus).

Soft Reset/EonTimer/Video Proofing

Since Soft Resets and EonTimer do not use any sort of overlay to verify that a target was achieved, you must provide a short, uninterrupted 10-15 second video of you encountering or redeeming the Pokémon in question. You may provide gif proofs instead of screenshots/videos if you want. This rule applies across all generations.

Short videos can be uploaded to Imgur, while longer videos can be uploaded to YouTube.

Pokémon from official Smogon Giveaways must provide a link from the official source.
