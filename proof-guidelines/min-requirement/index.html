Proofing Guidelines

This page contains an overview of the types of high value Pokémon that need proof to trade in the Wi-Fi room. Read through all of the information carefully if you plan to trade high value Pokémon.

What is proof?

Proof is used to help verify that a Pokémon has been legitimately obtained by a player and generally comes in the form of a video, or a complilation of pictures. Requiring proofing for specific high value Pokémon is the PS Wi-Fi way of reassuring the community about the legitimacy of the Pokémon received in trades from community members.

To decide if a Pokémon requires proof, we look at two main factors, rarity and exclusivity. IV count, shiny odds, and other game specific information are all taken into account to make an informed decision.

It is advised to partially blur sensitive information on your proof screenshots and videos (such as initial seeds) to prevent people from stealing your proof and / or cloning your Pokémon.

About IV spreads:

The amount of flawless IVs a Pokémon has will often be the determined factor that decides if it needs proof. The Wi-Fi room considers IVs of 0, 1, 30 and 31 as "flawless", or "perfect". There are also unique cases that are listed here.

IV spreads are in the sequential order of HP/ATK/DEF/SPA/SPD/SPE An unknown IV value can be listed as X in tradesheets, giveaway forms, etc.

In all cases, it is advised to unlock the IV judge in any game it exists before attempting to prove a Pokémon. This will make things significantly easier. If you are unable to use the IV judge, you must find an alternative method to definitively prove the IVs your Pokémon has, such as using an online IV calculator, or Showdown Teambuilder.

IN LEGENDS: ARCEUS: Only full 31 IVs will be considered towards the total IV count. An effort level value of 3 on the summary screen, after initial capture is a "full" 31 IV.

Examples of flawless IV spreads:
Physical Attackers:
  • 31/31/31/x/31/31 | Perfect for a physical attacker.
  • 31/31/31/x/31/0 | Perfect for a physical attacker in Trick Room.
Special Attackers:
  • 31/0/31/31/31/31 | Perfect for a special attacker.
  • 31/0/31/31/31/0 | Perfect for a special attacker in Trick Room.
Unique Examples:
  • 0/0/0/0/0/0 | Meme spread, typically found on Ditto; this would contribute a total of 6 perfect IVs.
  • 31/31/15/x/31/0 | 5 perfect IVs + Lonely nature; Trick Room Stakataka optimized for Beast Boost to boost Attack.
  • 3/3/3/3/3/3 | 6 perfect Effort Levels in Legends: Arceus. An effort level value of 3 after initial capture is the same as a full 31 IV.
Disclaimers:
  1. The proofing guidelines are not up for debate in the Wi-Fi room.

    2. If you have a strong argument that the proofing guidelines are lacking, or that a certain section should be revisited, contact a member of the Room Staff team in private messages to explain your reasoning.

  2. You must provide proof upon staff member request.
    3. Failure to provide proof for Pokémon that meet the minimum proof requirement may result in room punishment.

Does my Pokémon need proof?

This section of the page covers the types of Pokémon that require proof in each generation. If your Pokémon meets the standard of anything listed below, you must be able to provide proof to trade them in our room.

All Generations:

Pokémon obtained from Wild/Roaming/Static Encounters & Gift Redemptions:

Encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 3 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny Pokémon
Encounters with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 5 IV+ shiny Pokémon
  • 6 IV non-shiny Pokémon

Pokémon obtained from Event Distributions:

Distributions with 0 guaranteed IVs
  • 4 IV+ non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Distributions with 3 guaranteed IVs
  • 6 IV non-shiny/shiny Pokémon
Other
  • "Sometimes shiny" shiny event Pokémon
Note: Event Pokémon with with fixed values (IVs, Nature, etc), such as guaranteed 6IV shiny Eternatus or HOME Melmetal, do not need proof.

TSV hatched Pokémon:

  • Pokémon obtained through hatching a shiny Pokémon due to matching TSV values traded between two users require proof.
  • TSV hatched Pokémon must provide a screenshot of the conversation between the two users, agreeing upon a TSV hatch.

If your Pokémon meets the description of anything listed inside above, it will need proof to be traded inside the PS! Wi-Fi room. Continue reading down below for other game-specific proof requirements.
Generation 8/9:

This includes: Scarlet/Violet, Sword/Shield, Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Let's Go! Pikachu/Let's Go! Eevee, & Legends: Arceus.

  • Shiny Pokémon from 1% chance encounters.
  • Anything else listed inside the All Generations box.

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from wild "rare tera" encounters.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from wild "rare tera" encounters.
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Tera Raid dens.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from Tera Raid dens.

Sword/Shield:

  • Shiny Pokémon from a Wild encounter with a Rare Mark.
  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from promotional Max Raid Dens with a boosted shiny chance.

Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl:

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from PokéRadar chains.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from PokéRadar chains.

Let's Go! Pikachu/Eevee:

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • 6 IV Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • Wild Shiny Articuno, Zapdos, and Moltres from Overworld-based encounters.

Legends Arceus:

  • 5 IV+ Shiny Pokémon from encounters in the Overworld, Space-Time Distortions, or (Massive) Mass Outbreaks.
  • Flawless Legendary and Mythical Pokémon.

Untradable Pokémon

The following Pokémon cannot be traded in the Wi-Fi room at all:

  • Pokémon from in-life event distributions in Generation 3.
  • 6 IV event Pokémon from in-life distributions in Generation 8.
  • 6 IV shiny Pokémon from eggs obtained in Link Trades in Generation 8.
  • All shiny Pokémon from event/promotional Watchtower Max Raid distributions.
  • 6IV shiny rare form Tandemaus and Dudunsparce from wild encounters or hatched from eggs in Generation 9.
  • (Legends: Arceus only) 6 IV non-shiny Pokémon from overworld encounters, Space-Time Distortions, and (Massive) Mass Outbreaks. This is due to insufficient methods of proofing these Pokémon.
