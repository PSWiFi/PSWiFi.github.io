Proofing Guidelines

“Proof" refers to pictures or videos that show a Pokémon being obtained by a player and helps to reduce the chance that a Pokémon is hacked. Proof of Pokémon you trade should be readily available upon staff request.

The Wi-Fi room has minimum proof requirements for certain "rare" Pokémon. These requirements can vary depending on IVs and Shininess, as well as the generation or game the Pokémon was caught in.

It is advised to partially blur sensitive information on your proof screenshots and videos to prevent people from stealing your proof and/or cloning your Pokémon. You may partially blur initial seeds and PIDs to avoid this.

Wi-Fi’s Proofing Guidelines are not up for debate in the Wi-Fi room.

If you have a strong argument that our proof guidelines are lacking or that a section of it should be revisited, kindly PM a member of our staff team (% and up). The final decision remains at the discretion of Wi-Fi staff.


Examples of "Flawless" or "Perfect" IV spreads:

  • 31/31/31/x/31/31 Adamant (5 IV), perfect IVs for a physical attacking Pokémon.
  • 31/0/31/31/31/31 Timid (6 IV), perfect IVs for a special attacking Pokémon.
  • 31/0/31/30/31/30 Timid (6 IV, HP Fire)
  • 31/1/31/31/31/0 Quiet (6 IV, HP Ice, Trick Room)
  • 31/30/30/x/31/31 Adamant (5 IV, 30's are counted just like 31's)
  • 31/31/15/x/31/0 Lonely Stakataka (5 IV, Trick Room, optimized for Beast Boost)
  • 0/0/0/0/0/0 Gen 5 Ditto (6 IV, has terrible but unlikely meme IVs)
The Wi-Fi room defines 0, 1, 30 and 31 IVs as “Perfect”, and so a Pokémon will need proof if they meet the requirements. For example, a 31/1/31/31/31/0 Quiet Cresselia would be considered “6IV” or “Perfect” by our definition. (With the exception of Legends: Arceus)

IN LEGENDS: ARCEUS ONLY: Only full 31 IVs are considered towards total flawless IV count. This will be displayed by an initial effort level of 3 in a stat after capture.

Does my Pokémon need proof?

This portion of the document covers the types of Pokémon that require proof in each generation. If your Pokémon meets the standard of anything listed below you must be able to provide proof to trade them in our room.

All Generations
  • TSV (Trainer Shiny Value) hatched Pokémon obtained between two users.
  • 3IV+ Shiny Pokémon from wild, stationary or roaming encounters with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • 4IV+ Pokémon from wild, stationary or roaming with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • 4IV+ Non-shiny Pokémon from an event distribution with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • 5IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 3 guaranteed IVs.
  • 6IV Shiny locked/always shiny Pokémon from an event distribution with 3 guaranteed IVs.
  • 6IV Pokémon from a stationary encounter with 3 guaranteed IVs.
Generation 8:
This includes: Sword/Shield, Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl, Let's Go! Pikachu/Let's Go! Eevee & Legends: Arceus.

All Gen 8 Titles:
  • 5IV+ Pokémon from global event distribution codes with 3 guaranteed IVs.
  • 3IV+ Pokémon from global event distribution codes with 0 guaranteed IVs.
  • All Pokémon from unique code event distributions.
  • Shiny Pokémon from 1% chance encounters.
A Global event distribution hands out the same code for all players to redeem the Pokémon, this can be shared amongst players and reused, typically you can use these codes once per save file.

A Unique event distribution hands out unique codes for players to redeem Pokémon, these are one-time-use and cannot be shared around, they also typically require an in-life action to obtain one.

Event Pokémon, (such as HOME distributions) with fixed IVs/shininess do not need proof.

Sword/Shield Specifics:
  • Shiny Pokémon from a Wild Encounter with a Rare Mark.
  • 3IV+ Shiny Pokémon from a Wild Encounter.
  • 5IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
  • 6IV Pokémon from Max Raid Dens or Dynamax Adventures.
Shiny from limited time promotional dens with boosted shiny chance only need proof if they have 6 flawless IVs.

Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl Specifics:
  • 5IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Pokeradar chains.
  • 6IV Pokémon from Pokeradar chains.
Let's Go! Pikachu/Let's Go! Eevee Specifics:
  • 5IV+ Shiny Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • 6IV Pokémon from Catch Combo chains.
  • Wild Shiny Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres from Overworld based encounters.
Legends: Arceus Specifics:
  • 5IV+ Shiny Pokémon from encounters in the Overworld, Space Time Distortions or (Massive) Mass Outbreaks.
  • Flawless Legendary and Mythical Pokémon (Flawless is considered perfect 5IV+ in Legends: Arceus)

Soft Reset/Eon Timer/Video Proofing/TSV Hatches

Since SR and Eontimer do not use any sort of overlay to verify that a target was achieved, you must provide a short, uninterrupted 10-15 second video of you encountering or redeeming the Pokémon in question. You may provide gif proofs instead of screenshots/videos if you want. This rule applies across all generations.

Short videos can be uploaded to Imgur, while longer videos can be uploaded to YouTube.

Pokémon from official Smogon Giveaways must provide a link from the official source.

TSV hatched Pokémon must provide a screenshot of the conversation between the two users, agreeing upon a TSV hatch.
