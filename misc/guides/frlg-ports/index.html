Fire Red & Leaf Green Ports

Pokémon Fire Red & Leaf Green were released as standalone titles on the Nintendo Switch on February 27th, 2026. As these games differ significantly from the modern Pokémon experience, this page provides helpful tips and tricks to enhance your overall gameplay experience and help you adjust to their mechanics.

1. Shiny Pokémon

Shiny Pokémon are much rarer in the older generations. In Generation 3 the odds are 1 in 8,192, unlike later games where the rates of finding a Shiny Pokémon are significantly increased. There are no ways to increase your shiny rates in these games, no chaining methods, no Shiny Charm, nothing. Every encounter has the same probability. On the bright side, shiny locks do not exist, hunt that Shiny Mewtwo!

2. Boat Tickets

The Mystic Ticket and Aurora Ticket were distributed at special in-person events on the original releases of Fire Red / Leaf Green to grant access to special areas where players can capture Ho-Oh, Lugia and Deoxys. On the Switch ports these tickets are given to the player after defeating the Elite 4 and can be used by revisiting the docks/port in Vermillion City.

3. Fairy Type

The Fairy type did not exist until Generation 6, this means that the Generation 3 games do not include the Fairy type at all. Notably Cleffa, Clefairy, Clefable, Igglybuff, Jigglypuff, Wigglytuff, Mr. Mime, Togepi, Togetic, Marill, Azumarill, Snubbull, and Granbull retain their original typings and therefore do not have any Fairy-type weaknesses or resistances.

4. Steel Type Defensive Match-ups

Prior to the type match-up chart changes made in Generation 6, Steel-type Pokémon resist Ghost- and Dark-type attacks.

5. Exp. Share

Unlike Generation 6 onward, the Exp. Share is not automatically applied in Generation 3. Instead, it is a held item obtained later in the game and the effect only applies to the Pokémon holding it. If you want a Pokémon to gain experience you better make sure it participates in battle or holds the Exp. Share!

7. TMs, HMs & Move Tutors

TMs in Generation 3 can only be used once and very few can be found more than once in a game. When you acquire a TM, think carefully about which Pokémon should learn it. Chances are, you won't be getting another copy of it without an in-person trade.

HMs can be found throughout the game and taught to your Pokémon as many times as you like. However, keep in mind that once a Pokémon learns an HM, it cannot be forgotten normally. You'll need to visit the Move Deleter in Fuchsia City to remove it from their moveset. It's generally a good idea to have a Pokémon on your team that can learn as many HMs as possible to avoid having Pokémon you commonly use lose valuable move-slots.

Move tutors can be found throughout the game and will teach a Pokémon moves which were TMs in Generation 1 or are otherwise unobtainable - these are also one use only. So like TMs, think carefully.

8. Move Reminder

Unlike more recent games, relearning a move is quite a long process. You need a special NPC called the Move Reminder, who can't be accessed untill near the end of the game (Two Island). He requests two Tiny Mushrooms or a single Big Mushroom (often held by wild Paras and Parasect) per relearned move. These mushrooms are valuable in more ways than just money, do not sell them! Additionally, Pokémon cannot re-learn Egg Moves or moves taught by TM like they can in the modern games.

9. Strange Evolutions

In Fire Red / Leaf Green Pokémon will not evolve into later generation Pokémon until the National Pokédex is obtained. This involves beating the Elite 4 with 60 Pokémon obtained, then talking to Professor Oak.

The notable examples of Pokémon that do not evolve until the post game are Golbat and Chansey; even if the required happiness is obtained, they will not evolve. They will try to, but you will be presented with an strange "?" text box on screen and the evolution will be prevented.

In addition, due to the lack of time-based mechanics, Eevee cannot evolve into Espeon and Umbreon at all.

10. The Legendary Beasts

One of the Crowned Beasts (Suicune, Raikou, and Entei) are obtainable post game, and the one you get in your game decided is by whichever one is super-effective against your starter. They roam around like in Johto, however they have a pretty serious bug.

If you have Suicune you do not need to worry and can move on. Raikou and Entei however, will permanently despawn if they ever use the move Roar against you. This will normally only occur if you use Mean Look or an ability like Arena Trap. For this reason it is recommended to save your Master Ball for Raikou and Entei, or to save the game before setting out to hunt them.

11. The Rocket Game Corner

The Game Corner (openly run by organised crime syndicate Team Rocket) has different prizes depending on version. While these are minor for the most part, such as slight price changes and some version exclusives, there are more significant differences like the price of Porygon. While Porygon only costs 6,500 Coins in Leaf Green, in Fire Red it costs a whopping 9,999 coins!

That's an extra 35,000 Pokédollars to buy all those coins. As there's not a lot of ways to make quick money in older games, you might want to consider the cost of Porygon when deciding on which version to get.

12. Poison Out-of-battle Effect

In Generation 3 games, poison continues to cause damage outside of battle. Your Pokémon will lose 1 HP per 4 steps taken in the overworld, so you can easily black out walking along a route as the HP of your Pokémon ticks down. Hold onto those Antidotes, Pecha Berries, Full Heals and Full Restores!
