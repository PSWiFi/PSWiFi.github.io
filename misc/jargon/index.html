Commonly Used Terms & Phrases

Wondering what people mean when they say they're looking for a BBHA, SOS chained Pokémon? This page contains information about commonly used terms and phrases. Trading communities commonly use unique terminology and abbreviations when communicating with each other, this page will cover everything you need to know!

Flawless IVs Refers to a Pokémon with ideal IVs for competitive battles, usually 4-6 Perfect 31 IVs. IVs of 0 are considered flawless if in the correct place, for the correct Pokémon, such as a brave 0 speed Stakataka for Trick Room, or a special attacking Pokémon with 0 attack to take minimum damage from Foul Play.
Apricot Balls Specific Poké Ball types originally obtained from Apricorns in HGSS. They returned in later games, but became limited in quantities through natural gameplay. In Sword & Shield You can get more by beating the Champion's Cup or the Isle of Armor’s Cram-O-Matic. Examples include: Moon Ball, Fast Ball, and Friend Ball. Along with Apriballs, there are 4 more types of "Rare" Poké Ball: Dream Ball, Beast Ball, Sport Ball & Safari Ball, which similarly reappeared later games, but became limited in quantity.
Battle Ready (BR) Refers to a Pokémon that is ready for competitive, BR Pokémon have the correct level, IVs, EVs, nature, and moveset for competitive battling.
Breedable Any Pokémon that can be bred to produce eggs.
Breedjects A shortened form of "Breeding Rejects," this refers to any breedable Pokémon left over from breeding. They usually have imperfections in IVs, nature, ability, etc.
Brilliant Aura In the overworld, some Pokemon have a yellow-golden aura surrounding them. This is a Brilliant Aura Pokemon. Catching them gives you Watts, and Brilliant Aura Pokemon have guaranteed 2 IVs, and will also have an Egg Move. You can increase your chance to find Brilliant Aura Pokemon by catching more Pokemon of that same species.
Egg Moves (EMs) Moves that are passed onto offspring when a Pokémon is bred.
Effort Values (EVs) EVs increase the base stats of your Pokémon and are fundemental to building a strong competitive team, you can increase the EVs of your Pokémon by feeding it vitamins, or battling and defeating Pokémon of a specific species!
Effort Levels (ELs) ELs increase the base stats of your Pokémon in Legends: Arceus and are fundemental to making your Pokémon stronger! You can increase the ELs of your Pokémon by feeding it Grit, which you can obtain by releasing any unwanted Pokémon at the pasture!
Hidden Ability (HA) Refers to a Pokémon that has it's Hidden Ability.
Hidden Power The "HP" is an abbreviation of "Hidden Power," which is a move whose type is dependent on IV spread. Hidden Powers are no longer obtainable in gen 8.
Hyper Trained (HTed) In Generation 7 & 8, Hyper Training allows you to boost a Pokémon's IVs using bottle caps. A Hyper Trained IV behaves like it is 31 in battle, but breeding still uses the base IV. This allows Pokémon to have Hidden Powers without losing power by having imperfect IVs.
Illegal An illegal Pokémon is impossible to obtain through normal methods of playing the game. For example, a Cherish Ball Cosmog is sadly illegal because there is no game in which you can get a Cosmog in a Cherish Ball, no matter how much we all want one...
Illegitimate An illegitimate Pokémon is one that was not obtained through an intended or official source. The WiFi room considered clones to be legitimate as long as the original was legitimate. All hacked Pokémon are illegitimate even if they are legal because they were created or modified by external devices.
Imperfect Describes a Pokémon with any trait that makes it unsuitable for competitive use.
Individual Values (IVs) These are hidden numbers that range from 0 to 31 and affect a Pokémon's stats. Every stat has an IV and they are usually listed in the order of HP/Atk/Def/SpA/SpD/Spe. Usually, IVs only need to be stated if they are 0, 30, or 31; X can be used in place of unknown IVs.
Original Trainer (OT) The player name of the person who bred or caught the Pokémon. Found in the summary.
Secret ID (SID) A 4 or 5 digit identifier given to every player that appears in the data of any Pokémon caught or bred by them. This is a number that is used to calculate other values such as TSV and Generation 7 TID and is not visible through normal gameplay.
Shop Ball Refers to balls that can be purchased out of Poké Marts. Examples include the Luxury Ball, Great Ball, and Premier Ball.
Star & Square Shiny This refers to the sparkle animation your shiny Pokémon is given when sent out into battle in Sword & Shield, the effect is purely visual and the different shiny types have different rarities depending on the origin of your encounter.
Trainer ID (TID) Found on your Trainer Card in-game. In Generations 1-6, this is 5 digits and in Generations 7 & 8, this is 6 digits.
Virtual Console (VC) Refers to Pokémon transferred from the Generation 1 and 2 games re-released on 3DS.
